OZEV EV Chargepoint Grant 2026: who qualifies and how to claim.

Up to £350 off your home charger if you rent, own a flat, or run a business. Here's exactly who qualifies, what's changed, and how we handle the paperwork.

What is the OZEV EV Chargepoint Grant?

The OZEV EV Chargepoint Grant (formerly the Electric Vehicle Homecharge Scheme) is a UK government scheme that pays for part of the cost of installing a smart EV charger at a residential or commercial property.

Since April 2022 the residential grant has been restricted - owner-occupier homeowners no longer qualify. But the scheme still pays out for renters, flat owners and businesses, and the rules are clearer than they used to be.

How much is it worth?

  • £350 off your install - residential renters and flat owners (or 75% of the install cost, whichever is lower).
  • £350 per socket up to 40 sockets - businesses, under the Workplace Charging Scheme (WCS).
  • Up to £30,000 - landlords letting residential or commercial properties (separate landlord grant).

Who qualifies in 2026?

Renters & tenants

You qualify if you live in a rented residential property and have written consent from your landlord, dedicated off-street parking, and an electric or plug-in hybrid vehicle (owned, leased or on order).

Flat owners & leaseholders

If you own (freehold or leasehold) a flat or apartment, you qualify on the same terms as renters - off-street parking and an eligible EV.

Businesses (Workplace Charging Scheme)

UK-registered businesses, charities and public sector organisations qualify under WCS. The grant covers up to £350 per socket and up to 40 sockets per applicant. Sockets must be on dedicated off-street parking owned/leased by the applicant.

Owner-occupier homeowners (no longer eligible)

The residential grant for owner-occupier homeowners was withdrawn on 31 March 2022. Our 0% finance via Bumper softens the cost - from £25/mo, no deposit.

How do I claim?

  1. Choose an OZEV-approved installer. Plug In Stations is OZEV-approved for both residential and WCS.
  2. We submit the claim for you. No paperwork on your end - we handle everything with the DVLA / OZEV portal.
  3. The grant comes off your invoice. You don't pay the £350 yourself and wait for a refund - it's deducted from your install cost upfront.
  4. Install completes in 7-10 days. Standard install time across the UK.

What you'll need to provide

  • Proof of ID (driving licence or passport)
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • Vehicle V5C, lease agreement, or order confirmation
  • Renters: written landlord consent (we provide a template)

Frequently asked questions

Can I get the grant if I don't have my EV yet?
Yes. A signed order or lease agreement counts as proof of vehicle. The install must complete within 4 months of order date for residential applicants.
Does the grant cover the EV charger or the install?
Both. The £350 is applied to the total cost of the unit plus install plus certification.
What if my landlord won't agree?
We'll happily speak to your landlord on your behalf. Many landlords will say yes once we explain the install is fully reversible and increases property value.
Can I combine the grant with finance?
Yes. The grant comes off the install price, and the remainder can go on 0% finance via Bumper. We sort both at quote time.
When does the grant expire?
There's no public end-date for renters/flat owners or WCS in 2026 - but it's always government-backed funding, so claim sooner rather than later.
